VoxFlow app icon — four directions

Each direction shown in the macOS squircle on dark and light docks (between neighbor apps), the dock size ladder, and the monochrome menu-bar template glyph it implies. No liquid glass, no bubble highlights, no Wispr rainbow-wave. All vector, so the winner renders crisply at every size from 16 px to 1024 px.

A · Waveline — “speech becomes text”

One continuous monoline stroke: a voice waveform that resolves into a written baseline, ending in a cursor dot. This is the product story in a single line — dictation in, clean text out. Restrained ink ground, paper stroke, one teal signal at the cursor. Reads as deliberate, not generated.

B · V-bars — letterform identity

Seven waveform bars whose tops carve a V in negative space: simultaneously a sound meter and the V of VoxFlow. Warm paper ground with ink bars (an intentional contrast to every dark-gradient AI app), one indigo bar marking the active voice. Strongest small-size legibility of the four; most “brandable.”

C · Flow ribbons — duotone current

Two phase-shifted ribbons crossing like interleaved speech, in a single restrained teal-to-indigo gradient on near-black ink. The most “flow”-forward option; differentiated from Wispr by the two-color restraint and the crossing geometry (theirs is a multicolor single wave). Flat — no glow, no bubble.

D · Cursive v — the written voice

A single handwriting stroke: a lowercase cursive “v” whose entry vibrates like a waveform before settling into the letter. Where A says “wave becomes line,” D says “wave becomes writing.” Quietest and most literary of the four; gradient lives in the stroke itself (teal arriving, paper leaving), flat ink ground.
